About me

The beginning

In September of 2016 I followed some friends' advice to try out some front-end coding. I loved it! The problem-solving mindset, the creativity and attention to detail it requires, and the rapid progress within technology suited me well. It turned out to be such a perfect match that I left a stable job in contracts management at a pharmaceutical company in April of 2017, to learn and practise front-end development full-time.

Some months later, I got my first developer job as a JavaScript Engineer at an online casino company. After an overwhelming start that introduced several new technologies and concepts such as containerisation with Docker and Kubernetes, my interest and determination soon made the pieces fall into place bit by bit.

The present

However, as adventurous outdoor enthusiasts, my partner and I had considered moving abroad to the mountains for quite a while. Eventually we found a winner in Vancouver, Canada, and in June of 2019 we left Stockholm and Sweden behind to begin a new chapter in our lives. I look forward to getting to know Vancouver and taking another step towards becoming the best possible software developer I can be.

Are you looking to hire? Download my resume or send me a message! I'd love to see how I can contribute.

When I'm not coding I'm likely out exploring the city and its surroundings, solving crossword puzzles, out running, skiing or meeting friends.

Skills

Front-end technologies

I regularly work with the latest features of HTML, CSS, JavaScript and React.js. I am familiar with using Redux with React.js too. When writing unit tests I mainly use Jest with Enzyme, or React Testing Library.

Back-end technologies

I also have experience in developing and maintaining Node.js microservices. Either in combination with Restify and CouchDB, or with Express.js and Firebase. For unit testing in Node.js, Mocha and Chai are my standard.

User-centricity

As user perception and satisfaction are a constant focus in my work, I aim to build easy-to-use, fast and functional web apps. Navigation and features should be intuitive and clear to the user right away.

Structure
